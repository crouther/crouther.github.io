Design: Mobile & Web

A closer look into industry inspirations

    You know the funny thing is, if you knew anything about mobile design and its history you'd realize:

  1. Many companies borrow from each other
  2. Gestures, UI fragments, app drawers, web apps, and more all came from the "imaginers" at Palm
  3. Android manufacturers experiment with hardware features years before mainstream companies adopt their features for mainstream products

Palm's designs have slowly trickled their way into influencing every major platform for the last 10 years, and their system has been open sourced, defunct, and sold several times to many companies in a tale of mismanagement causing the fall of one of the mobile industry's giants.

Web:

But then when you look at the web it's quite similar in its spiral nature. Frameworks, "Web Components", WebObjects (remember those?). They are all essentially reinventions of themselves to solve the same problem. How do you display content to the user? How do you update that content consistently? How do you insure the consumer has a satisfying experience?

Steve Jobs did a talk on WebObjects which are like the 90's version of the webs latest trend, Web Components. At least conceptually as a spiritual successor of sorts. Sure, the functionality didn't exist in browser those days. But at that time, they were still relying on Java Applets and Browser Add-on's for animations and additional functionality.
